Becoming a coder

  • I studied Environmental Science and GIS at UCCS. In my last few semesters, I realized I wanted to code. I took 3 semesters of programming courses and through my journey, I decided web development was my favorite.
  • After I graduated, I began to teach myself some basics about front end development to get a feel for how it really worked. I realized at this rate though, the evolution of the market was outpacing my learning speed.
  • I decided I needed to look into some programming courses and came across a variety of technical schools in the Denver area dedicated to web development. After looking into a couple different school choices, I decided to get involved with a school called RefactorU and took my first step into becoming a web developer.
  • Once I graduated from RefactorU, I freelanced for a year, picking up random gigs and tutoring. A more stable source of income presented itself, and I joined small company down in Colorado Springs. During my time there, I gained some professional experience in a cozy work place.
  • I currently reside at the Colorado Springs Gazette as a Junior Developer. The environment is fast pace and has given me multiple opportunities to learn valuable skills, such as cross browser usability, deploying sites and project management.
